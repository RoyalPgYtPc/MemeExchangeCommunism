The Community Jury.

A new way for the community to weigh in on difficult calls. Not a mod role. Not a daily commitment. Just members helping guide fair outcomes when it matters.

Apply to the Jury How selection works
The role

What the
Jury does.

Lightweight, occasional, and focused on outcomes — not enforcement.

Vote on difficult decisions

When a call is genuinely hard — not a clear rule violation — Jury members vote to help reach a fair resolution.

Review edge-case situations

Posts and disputes that don't fit neatly into the rulebook get a closer look from people who actually use the sub.

Guide fair outcomes

Through discussion and polls, Jury members help the mod team land on decisions the community can actually trust.

This is NOT

  • A moderator position
  • A role with enforcement powers
  • A daily or heavy workload

This IS

  • Occasional, lightweight participation
  • Voting only — no actions taken directly
  • Open to any eligible member
The process

How selection
works.

Four steps from application to invitation.

1
Apply
Anyone can submit an application — fill in the form below. It takes a few minutes.
2
Eligibility check
To qualify you'll need 200+ community karma, an account that's been active in the subreddit, and a positive contribution history.
3
Review process
Applications are scored on activity & consistency, quality of contributions, and decision-making ability shown in your answers.
4
Invitation
Selected members are invited into the Jury group, where voting on real cases takes place.
